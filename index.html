The problem

You are excellent on a normal day. Then the pressure lands.

It has a name. "Checkitis" is when a fully competent professional delivers a degraded performance during an examination, a check ride or a high-stakes moment. The knowledge is there. The stress reaction gets in the way.

⚡

The startle reflex

A sudden failure or surprise triggers a reaction that hijacks clear thinking at the exact moment you need it most.

💬

Goal conflict

Multiple obligations collide under time pressure, and the resulting stress narrows your attention and shrinks your options.

🎯

Lost situational awareness

Under load you lose the big picture. Regaining it fast is a trainable skill, not a personality trait.

Your instructor

Captain Ray Werndly

Ray has spent more than 35 years in aviation, including 22 years as a flight instructor and examiner. He has sat on both sides of the check ride and watched brilliant professionals undone not by a lack of skill, but by their reaction to pressure.

He built the Performance Course in 2010 to close a gap that standard training left open. Since then it has helped over a thousand professional pilots, along with police, clinicians and leaders, walk into the highest-stakes moments of their careers with their full faculties available.
